Fee and Service Agreement — Benson Legal Services

Our commitment to you

Your supervisor will have their Child Abuse Clearances and PA and State Criminal Clearances. Our supervision is "eyes on" at all times. If the supervisor believes you are being unsafe with the child or the child is in duress, the visit will be cancelled. Future visits may still occur. You are responsible to bring any toys, games, food, or entertainment for the children. You are responsible to provide the supervisor with a list of your child's allergies or health issues. The supervisor will not provide assistance during the visit and will not give medication.

Conduct during visits

  • No alcohol, drugs, or arriving under the influence of any substance.
  • No physical discipline, threats, or yelling at the child.
  • Appropriate language must be used at all times.
  • The child is not to be questioned or discussed regarding the court case, custody matters, or the other parent.
  • The child may not be photographed, recorded, or asked to keep secrets.
  • There will be no whispering during visits.
  • No phone use during the visit unless previously approved.
  • No additional persons without prior court approval or approval from all parties.
  • Parties shall not communicate with each other before or after visits.

Visit times / arrivals and departures

The parent being supervised shall arrive 10 minutes earlier than the visit time. They will have the opportunity to set up the room and prepare. The supervised parent shall leave after the children and the other parent have left the building.

Gift giving and food

Gifts, food, or snacks may be brought only with prior approval by the supervisor or custodial parent. No items that are inappropriate, unsafe, or not age-appropriate. All items brought to the visit will be documented.

Payment of fees

All fees are nonrefundable. You are paying for a supervisor to be available for the visits. Cancelled, rescheduled, or early-ended visits will not be refunded. Past due balances: 1.5%/month after 30 days · $20.00 collection fee at 60 days · $35.00 returned check fee.

Costs incurred

You are responsible for any costs assumed by our company during your visit including cleaning, broken items, and travel expenses. All costs must be paid before the last visit can occur.

Reports and testimony

A report is provided after each visit to the party who paid. Court testimony requires a subpoena at least 14 days prior. Cost: $100/hr · $200 minimum retainer due at least 14 days before the hearing.

Recordings and photographs (18 Pa.C.S. § 5703)

By signing, you consent to recording of your voice, image, and likeness during visits. Recordings are used for reporting purposes only and shall not be disseminated to any non-employee or contractor of BLS. PA is a two-party consent state — this agreement satisfies that requirement.

Use of technology and artificial intelligence

BLS utilizes AI tools to assist in the delivery of services. All AI-assisted work product is reviewed by a licensed attorney before being relied upon. BLS takes reasonable steps to protect client confidentiality and will use AI tools configured not to use client information to train external systems.

Dispute resolution

All disputes shall be submitted to mandatory, binding arbitration in York County, Pennsylvania. You expressly waive your right to file a lawsuit in any civil court against Benson Legal Services, the supervisor, staff, or contractors.

Custody Exchange — Fee and Service Agreement

Fees

Rate: $75.00 per exchange. Mandatory intake fee: $100.00 per family unit — one time, paid before exchanges are scheduled. All fees are nonrefundable. Cancellations with less than 24 hours notice, or if the monitor attends and the exchange does not occur for any reason, the full $75.00 exchange fee is forfeited. Repeated cancellations or no-shows may result in discontinued services. Court testimony: $100/hr · $200 minimum retainer due at least 14 days before the hearing.

Service description

The monitor provides a safe, neutral, conflict-free exchange of the child between parties and documents the interaction. The monitor does not take sides, make legal decisions, or interfere in custody disputes.

Conduct during exchanges

  • No alcohol, drugs, or arriving under the influence.
  • No verbal altercations, threats, or hostile behavior between parties.
  • No discussion of legal matters, custody disputes, or court proceedings in front of the children.
  • No derogatory comments about the other party in front of the children.
  • Parties shall arrive on time. Repeated tardiness may result in discontinued services.
  • No additional persons present without prior approval of all parties.
  • Follow all direction from the monitor without argument.

Documentation

The monitor will prepare a written report of each exchange including arrival times, demeanor of parties, and any notable incidents. Reports are provided to the party who has paid for services.

Recording consent (18 Pa.C.S. § 5703)

By signing, you consent to recording of your voice, image, and likeness during exchanges as part of the exchange report. PA is a two-party consent state — this agreement satisfies that requirement.

Dispute resolution

All disputes shall be submitted to mandatory binding arbitration in York County, PA. You expressly waive your right to file a lawsuit in any civil court against Benson Legal Services, monitors, staff, or contractors.

Home Study Fee Agreement — Sydney C.H. Benson, Esquire / Benson Legal Services

Fees

Home Study Flat Fee: $750.00 — paid via LawPay (operating account). BLS will send you the LawPay payment link after your intake meeting. Your home visit will be scheduled once payment is received.

Testimony Retainer: $200.00 — $100/hr · minimum $200 due at least 14 days before testimony. If the fee is not paid, testimony will not take place. If scope expands beyond the original estimate, the firm reserves the right to request additional funds. You will be contacted before any additional fees are charged.

Process

Once payment is received, a visit to your home will be scheduled. All adult household members must be present. We will need access to all areas of the home and ask that dogs be crated if they may become territorial and/or aggressive. We will review the reason the home study has been requested prior to conducting the visit and will specifically examine any areas of concern raised by parties, counsel, or the Court. The finalized report will be distributed in accordance with the Court order. Fee includes: initial consultation/interview · review of docket information · visit to the home · contact with up to 2 adult references (if requested) · final report distributed per Court order. Pursuant to Pennsylvania R.C.P. 1915.8(b), if any party wishes to enter the final report into evidence, the author of the report must be subject to cross-examination.

Information & Confidentiality

Email communications may not be privileged. We communicate only with the named client regarding case status — no updates to outside parties. Virtual meeting recording consent is granted by signing this agreement, valid until completion of your matter.

Independent Contractor Agreement — Benson Legal Services

1.1 Nature of services

Contractor will perform services for BLS as an independent contractor. Services are "work made for hire" under U.S. copyright law. Contractor grants BLS the right to use their name, voice, signature, photograph, likeness, and biographical information in connection with the services and their promotion.

1.2 Relationship of the parties

Contractor enters into this Agreement as, and shall continue to be, an independent contractor. Contractor shall not look to BLS as employer, partner, agent, or principal. Contractor is not entitled to employee benefits including worker's compensation, disability insurance, vacation, or sick pay. Contractor is responsible for all insurance, clearances, licenses, and permits necessary for conducting the services. Contractor shall not solicit or accept employment — by contract or as an employee — with any clients previously or currently working with BLS or Benson Legal Services. Contractor must immediately inform BLS if there is any investigation or finding that puts their clearances or necessary licenses at risk.

1.3 Compensation

Contractor shall be compensated per role as set forth below. No additional fees will be paid unless approved in advance in writing. Contractor is solely responsible for all taxes, Social Security contributions, disability insurance, unemployment taxes, and other payroll-type taxes. Contractor hereby indemnifies and holds BLS harmless from any claims arising from Contractor's failure in this section.

1.4 Confidential information

All BLS Information — including trade secrets, customer lists, techniques, and proprietary information — is strictly confidential. Contractor shall not disclose BLS Information to any third party at any time during or after the term of this Agreement.

1.8 Protection of client information

Contractor shall keep all client names, contact information, personal history, and medical information strictly confidential at all times during and after performance of services.

3.4 Representations and warranties

Contractor warrants: (a) full authority to enter this Agreement; (b) no violation of third-party agreements; (c) work product is original and does not infringe any third-party rights. Contractor shall defend and indemnify BLS from any claims arising from breach of these representations.

4.1 Term and termination

Agreement is terminable by either party at any time with or without cause. If BLS terminates, Contractor is compensated for work performed up to the time of termination. Agreement automatically terminates upon Contractor's death.

5.1 Governing law

Governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. Exclusive jurisdiction and venue in the federal and state courts sitting in York County, Pennsylvania.
